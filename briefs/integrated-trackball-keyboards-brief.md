### H2: What Is an Integrated Trackball Keyboard? (300–350 words)

- **Answer-first:** An integrated trackball keyboard is a keyboard with a trackball built directly into its housing — typically under one thumb — so you can type and control your cursor without ever moving your hands. Unlike a separate trackball mouse sitting next to your keyboard, the pointing device is part of the keyboard itself.
- Define the three 2026 approaches:
  1. **True integration** — trackball built into keyboard housing (Charybdis, Svalboard)
  2. **Magnetic bolt-on** — trackball module that attaches to an existing keyboard (ZSA Navigator)
  3. **External bar** — trackball device positioned between keyboard halves (Keychron Nape Pro)
- Brief history: from 1990s industrial Cortron/iKey boards to the modern ergonomic split keyboard movement.
- **Image:** Side-by-side comparison of all three approaches (product photos or illustrated diagram).

### H2: Why Does an Integrated Trackball Matter for Ergonomics? (400–500 words)

- **Answer-first:** "Split keyboards reduce ulnar deviation from 12 degrees to under 5 degrees of neutral (Marklin et al., Human Factors, 1999). Adding an integrated trackball eliminates the remaining ergonomic weak point — the shoulder-straining reach to a separate mouse."
- Cover three biomechanical problems solved:
  1. **Ulnar deviation** — wrists angling outward on a standard keyboard (cite Marklin et al.)
  2. **Forearm pronation** — standard mice force ~65.6 degrees of pronation; trackballs allow near-neutral position (cite CDC/NIOSH, 2015)
  3. **Shoulder abduction** — reaching for a mouse forces the arm away from the body; integrated trackball keeps hands in typing position
- RSI prevalence context: 9% of U.S. adults report RSI, wrist/hand is #1 area at 36.8% (CDC NHSR 189, 2023)

### FAQ Section (5 items with FAQPage schema)

Each answer should be 60-80 words — self-contained for AI extraction.

1. **Can a trackball keyboard completely replace a mouse?**
   Yes, for most knowledge work. An integrated trackball handles cursor movement, scrolling (via drag-scroll), and clicking. QMK/Vial firmware lets you map all mouse buttons to keyboard keys. The main exceptions are precision design work (CAD, Photoshop pixel-level editing) and competitive gaming, where a high-DPI mouse still has an edge.

2. **Are trackball keyboards better for carpal tunnel syndrome?**
   Trackball keyboards address two CTS risk factors: they eliminate the repetitive wrist extension from mouse gripping, and split models reduce ulnar deviation from 12 degrees to under 5 degrees (Marklin et al., Human Factors). CTS affects 7.8% of computer workers who type 4+ hours daily (Frontiers in Public Health, 2024). Consult a medical professional for diagnosis.

3. **What is the best split keyboard with a built-in trackball?**
   The Bastard Keyboards Charybdis MK2 is the only prebuilt keyboard combining a concave sculpted keywell with an integrated thumb trackball, open-source QMK/Vial firmware, and a manufacturer warranty. The ZSA Voyager + Navigator module offers a flatter, lighter alternative with a magnetic trackball attachment. Choice depends on whether you want a sculpted keywell.

4. **How long does it take to get used to a trackball keyboard?**
   Most users reach basic comfort in 1-2 weeks and full proficiency in 4-6 weeks. The adaptation involves two simultaneous changes: a split keyboard layout and thumb-trackball control. Starting with just the trackball (before switching keyboards) can reduce the learning curve. Typing speed typically recovers to 80% within 2 weeks.

5. **What's the difference between a trackball keyboard and a trackpad keyboard?**
   A trackball uses a physical ball rotated by the thumb for cursor control — offering high precision and no flat surface requirement. A trackpad (like the Bastard Keyboards Dilemma) uses a flat touch surface with gesture support — better for multi-finger gestures like pinch-to-zoom. Trackballs suit pointing-heavy work; trackpads suit gesture-heavy workflows.
