# Bastard Keyboards — Brand Reference

## Overview

**Bastard Keyboards** (bastardkb.com) is a small independent keyboard manufacturer founded in 2019, based in Dordrecht, The Netherlands. They specialize in split ergonomic keyboards built on an open-source philosophy. All products are handcrafted in their Netherlands atelier and shipped internationally.

**Tagline:** Open-source Ergonomic Innovation

---

## Brand Voice & Tone

- **Blunt and direct.** Confrontational framing to establish authority: "If you spend most of your day typing, your keyboard is slowly making things worse."
- **Technically confident but accessible.** Explains biomechanical concepts (ulnar deviation, pronation, columnar stagger) without drowning in jargon. Uses analogies like the "handshake position."
- **No-nonsense and honest.** Openly describes workarounds as "not recommended and overall terrible." Advertises "real human support" explicitly contrasting with AI support.
- **Irreverent.** The brand name is a deliberate provocation — signals they are not trying to be mass-market.
- **Community-anchored.** "We don't guess: we listen." Discord with 5,000+ members that genuinely influences hardware/firmware direction.

**Personality archetype:** The technically brilliant friend who tells you the truth even when it's uncomfortable, but builds things that genuinely work.

---

## Target Audience

1. **Knowledge workers with RSI / existing pain.** Programmers, writers, developers, data professionals who type several hours a day.
2. **Keyboard enthusiasts and DIY builders.** Comfortable with soldering, QMK firmware, reading GitHub docs.
3. **Open-source advocates.** The "open-source forever" commitment is a hard value signal — full 3D models, PCB schematics, BOMs, and firmware are all public.
4. **Risk-averse professionals wanting prebuilt.** Drawn to warranties, repairability, and "ships in 1 week."
5. **Experienced ergonomic keyboard users.** Their 35-key board is explicitly flagged as "for experienced users already comfortable with layers."

## Unique Selling Points

1. **Only prebuilt combining concave keywell + integrated thumb trackball.** No competitor offers this combination with a warranty and dedicated support.
2. **Open-source forever.** All hardware files on GitHub — Charybdis repo has 1,701 stars / 125 forks.
3. **Built to last.** 2–3 year warranties, repairability as a design principle.
4. **Real human support.** No AI chatbots — explicitly stated differentiator.
5. **EU manufacturing, fast lead times.** Handcrafted in Netherlands, kits ship in 1 week.
6. **Firmware flexibility.** Supports QMK (full code) and VIA (no-code GUI). RP2040 chip offers headroom over older ATmega controllers.
7. **Community as infrastructure.** Discord actively shapes firmware and hardware development.

## Content Strategy Notes

- Uses long-form SEO content (3,500+ word guides) targeting informational intent before conversion. E.g., "The Complete Guide to Split Ergonomic Keyboards."
- Self-positions as the answer to the Dactyl Manuform DIY journey — acknowledges that community, then offers the supported, warrantied, complete version.
- Brand name deliberately filters audience: attracts people who find it funny or bold, repels those looking for a corporate ergonomic brand.
- Stock availability issues on several products suggest demand exceeds production capacity — common for small-batch artisan hardware.
